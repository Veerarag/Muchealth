What to know about diabetic Neuropathy? NEW LANDING PAGE Diabetic neuropathy is a complication of diabetes that results in damage to thenervous system which worsens over time.
Four main types of neuropathies
  • Peripheral symmetric neuropathy: The most common type of neuropathy that affects the feet and hands.
  • Autonomic neuropathy: Occurs in the nerves that control involuntary functions of the body, such as digestion, urination, or heart rate
  • Thoracic and lumbar root, or proximal, neuropathy: This is damage to more focused areas of the body such as legs, chest wall area. Not widely distributed.
  • Mononeuropathies: These damages are specific to any individual nerves
What to look out for peripheral neuropathy?
  1. Are you feeling any numbness or tingling sensation starting in the toesand fingers then continuing up the legs or arms?
  2. Are you feeling any numbness or tingling sensation starting in the toesand fingers then continuing up the legs or arms?
  3. Are you having loss of balance?
What to look out for autonomic neuropathy?
  1. Do you feel nauseated?
  2. Are you experiencing constipation or diarrhea?
  3. Are you experiencing any difficulties speaking or swallowing?
  4. Are you vomiting several hours after eating?
  5. Do you feel light-headed / dizzy when standing up?
  6. Is your heart beating faster than normal?
  7. Are experiencing excessive sweating, even in cool temperatures or whileat rest?
  8. Are you experiencing difficulty emptying the bladder completely whenurinating / having incontinence?
  9. Are you having sudden muscle contractions / weakness?

What to know about diabetic Retinopathy? NEW LANDING PAGE
Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is an eye condition that occurs due to diabetes. It is the leading cause of blindness in adults and one of the main reasons for vision loss in adults. If you are diagnosed with diabetes, it is important to have a dilated eye exam yearly to look for any early signs of vision complications for early treatment.

What to look out for?
  1. Are you experiencing blurred vision?
  2. Are you experiencing blurred vision?
  3. Do you experience seeing frequent eye floaters?
  4. Do you experience seeing frequent eye floaters?
  5. Do you see a dark empty spot at the centre of your vision?
  6. Do you see a dark empty spot at the centre of your vision?
Short-term blurriness
  • Happens when fluid moves in and out of the eye due to uncontrolled high sugar levels. This causes the lens to swell leading to blurry vision.
Long-term
  • Prolonged levels of uncontrolled diabetes will lead to high blood sugar levels that damage small blood vessels over time. This damage can lead to complications to the retina of the eye which can result in blurry vision and eventually blindness.
When to see a doctor?
  1. If you are experiencing any of the above-mentioned symptoms, consult adoctor immediately
  2. Anyone who already has a diabetes diagnosis should go for a routine eye examination at least once a year. Regular tests can detect any problems whilethey are still minor.
Complications of Diabetic Retinopathy?

When blood vessels bleed into the main jelly that fills the eye,this is called vitreous haemorrhage. Severe cases this will lead to vision loss, as the blood in the vitreous blocks light from entering the eye. If the retina remains undamaged, bleeding in the vitreous can resolve itself. However, when diabetic retinopathy leads to a detached retina this will result in severe vision loss due to the scar tissue pulling the retina away from the eye causing blindness. A detached retina presents a significant risk of total vision loss if a person does not get treatment.

The normal flow of fluid in the eye may become blocked as new blood vesselsformation. This blockage causes a build-up of pressure within the eye, increasing the risk of nerve damage and vision loss.

How diabetes affects Sexual Health? NEW LANDING PAGE

High blood sugar levels can cause nerve damage throughout the body.

In Women:
  • Decreased ability to experience sexual stimulation / arousal
  • Reduced release of vaginal lubricant due to reduced nerve sensitivity
  • Painful sex
  • Reduced ability to experience an orgasm
In Men:
  • Inability to achieve or maintain an erection (Erectile Dysfunction)
  • Performance Anxiety due to fear of sexual failure
  • Reduced Sex Drive
  • Decreased Libido

Skin Conditions related to Diabetes? NEW LANDING PAGE

Skin complications from uncontrolled sugar levels are usually the first visible sign of diabetes. Proper management of blood sugar levels is usually the best way to prevent and treat skin problems that are due to diabetes. High levels of blood sugar will cause you to urinate more leading to dehydration and dry skin.
High blood sugar can lead to dehydration, dry skin, and inflammation

What do you need to look out for?
  1. Are you having sudden dry skin?
  2. Did you notice any dark patches of skin? mainly near skin folds?
  3. Are you experiencing frequent bacterial infections over the eyelids? such as styes or boils?
  4. Are you experiencing severe itching over your skin?
